-A user experience research plan made for future use-

BrightCrowd is a professional networking platform using artificial intelligence that provides connection among people who share similar interests. The goal of the research is to improve the user experience with more intuitive interaction; the objective is to increase user sustainability and satisfaction.

My Roles

Skill Set: Independent Research | Interview | UX Research Plan Writing

I talked to ten users to find the pain points and made the hypothesis for future research. Ans also provide the cost and time estimation for the user research.

Findings

10-15 minutes interviews with 10 urrent users allow me to find several problems brightcrowd platform currently have:

1. Registration is simple, but the instruction of the steps after can be improved to highlight the mission of the platform.

2. The responses and connections are real and instant, but the sustainability can be improved to keep user engagement.

3. The UI is clean and simple, but the interaction design can be improved to decrease confusion.

Hypothesis

Core Problem: Many inactive users stopped using after registration because their needs for maintaining practical networks is not meet by the current product.

Research Questions:What are the goals that users want to achieve by using BrightCrowd?

  1. Why do people stop using it after registration?
  2. What are usersâ€™ key expectations of BrightCrowd?
  3. How would users like BrightCrowd to play a role in helping them continue or develop connections made?

Methods

  1. Stakeholders Interviews: including 5 engineers and 1 marketing person to understand how the internal team regard the current stage of the product
  2. Comparative Analysis: including socioal networking media to see the strength and weakness the current product has
  3. User Interviews: including around 10 interviewees to understand verify the hypothesis I made earlier
  4. Survey: targeting on current user and correcting 60 responses to have quantitative proof the findings from user interviews
  5. Usability Testing: including 6 participants to further improve the current platform

Reflection

This was the first time I wrote a research plan. And while planning, I found it easily to forget the reason why we were doing the research and what problems we were trying to solve. The scope was hard to define. Many times people know there is a problem but may not be able to clearly articulate the question. Therefore, as a UX researcher in planning this research, I should always keep the key question in mind and avoid to be distracted.
