January to April, 2017

-User Research Project-

Expedia's services are currently being primarily used for shopping and buying phases. The company hopes to expand their services to be used for the trip planning stages in the near future. In order to help Expedia develop the related features, our project goal is to collect data for the planning process and improve the User Generated Content (UGC) access and usability.

My Role

Skill Set: Interaction Map | Interview | Comparative Analysis | Survey | Heuristic Evaluation | Persona | Scenario | Usability Testing | Qualitative Data Analysis

I worked on this project with another 4 students in the course called "Usability Testing." I went through the whole research process and wrote key findings and recommendations for the reports of Interview, Comparative Analysis, Survey, and Heuristic Evaluation, and wrote the methodology for Usability Testing report.

Interview

We conducted 7 interviews in total and analyzed the data from the 6 of them. I personally conducted 3 interviews with millennial travelers. At this stage, we discovered the tools users frequently used for planning trips, and the problems the travelers encountered while using online tools to plan their trip. We built the personas accordingly.

One of the personas, "Cindy"

Comparative Analysis

We analyzed 8 competitors by comparing the features and UI patterns of each app interface.

Example: Q9- How many reviews do you read before booking an activity?

Survey

We wrote about 40 survey questions and extracted 15 questions by discussing with Expedia to assure we could get the data they want, which is to understand the roles that UGC play in pre-booking and in-trip activity planning.

Usability Testing

We conducted usability tests with 5 millennial travelers on TripAdvisor to understand millennials planning a trip behavior using mobile travel application, so Expedia can learn from our findings when developing the features of trip planning.

Reflection

The project is still on-going. So far by doing this project, I understand how those UX research techniques are applied in the real world. For instance, I understand that not only doing the comparative analysis can help a company to understand the current products exist in the market, but conducting a usability testing on a competitor is also a good way to learn and improve their design.
