January to April, 2016

-An independent study to solve organizational problem with contextual inquiry-

Michigan Advocacy Program (MAP) and Michigan PLP, LLC were cooperating to provide legal services since 2014, but while the organizations are growing, the communication becomes inefficient so they try to seek consultation.

My Roles

Skill Set: Independent Research | Contextual Interview | Affinity Mapping | Presentation

I worked on this project voluntarily and planned the research, set the schedule and conducted and analyzed 6 interviews individually. I presented the findings and recommendations to the client on April 26, 2016.

Approaches

I conducted six interviews with people in two organizations, two of the interviewees are from the LLC and the other four are from the MAP.

Discoveries

Core Problem: Organizational issues instead of communicational issues

"I don't know when is the end of the discussion."
--Member A

"I am not sure who should be the decision maker in case X."
--Member B

"I have made the decision but the discussion keeps going and there is no end."
--Member C

The decision makeing power is unclear when the people have different idea about the structure of the organizations.

Solutions and Feedback

Solutions

  1. The heads of the two organizations need to discuss and define the relationship between the two institutions and convey to everyone else.

  2. The heads of the two organizations need to discuss and define the relationship between the two institutions and convey to everyone else.

Feedback

"Your work inspired good conversation between us and was very helpful!"
--the Client

Reflection

It was very challenging to plan, conduct, and analyze six contextual interviews on my own. I encountered challenges such as doing affinity diagrams alone. Categorizing the notes without being biased when I stared at them for a long time was hard. Besides research skills, I learned about logistics and communication with the clients. I would like to thank professor Kentaro Toyama from the UMSI for being my advisor and supporting me for this individual project.
